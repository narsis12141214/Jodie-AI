## What Worked

1. **First paid client closed (Haleh, 11 May)** — agency model proved real.
2. **Kish recovery deal LIVE + 10+ bookings captured in 2-3 days** — most important signal of the window. Of the 10+, two are unambiguously recovered (outside business hours) and the remaining ~8 came in during operating hours. Kish runs AI-first routing (no human-attempt-first), so the inclusive recovery definition does not apply to this client; only the outside-hours subset counts as anchor-eligible. Strictly extrapolated: 2 outside-hours recoveries in 2-3 days projects to ~20 per month, comfortably above the 10-per-month anchor on the strict definition alone. Product works in production; anchor not yet "hit" by mechanism but easily hit by extrapolation.
3. **Heny aesthetic clinic verbally closed (21 May)** — second segment opened.
4. **Strategic framework rebuild itself** — cross-segment pricing, performance anchor, off-ramp logic, cold email infrastructure (12 templates × 4 segments), contract signing protocol, Click Voice tier extension, Arman dev brief, Heny contract template, Lovable rebuild (5 landing pages live), photography brand rebuild framework (Maddie Mae). This is the asset that makes the next 60 days different.
5. **Photography SEO — corrected diagnosis** — N8N v2 fix revealed the real picture (533 keywords, 0.40% CTR). CTR not rankings is the lever.
6. **Mozhgan booking** — repeat client signal that the photography brand strength is still real.

## What Did Not Work — and Why

1. **Pipeline volume vs realistic close rate.** Target was 10 clients. Even at 100% close, the active pipeline of ~15 conversations was always going to fall short. Math was wrong from Day 1.
2. **Cold email automation took 6+ weeks to build.** Live week 7. 17 days left in window. Infrastructure pays off in Window 2.
3. **Verbal yes to signed contract leak.** Four deals stalled (Randeszvous, Maria, Kish initially, Ace). Root cause: time gap between yes and signature. Sign-in-the-room protocol adopted 12 May but only Heny was reached.
4. **Photography content rebuild paused ~2 weeks.** Click Desk dev consumed bandwidth. Acceptable trade-off, but explains the SEO position drops.
5. **"We call them back" objection.** 4-of-4 restaurant meetings raised price. Three reframes captured. Full objection document not built.
6. **Some leads should have been killed earlier.** Maria reviewed 6+ weeks. Ace silent 12+ days post-verbal-yes. Bandwidth waste.
7. **@hadyyazdani pause cost compounding follower growth.** Floor of 20K never reached.
8. **Maddie Mae methodology not adopted until Day 58.** Photography brand rebuild framework arrived as window closed. Phase 1 will lock in Window 2.

## Strategic Insights From the Window

1. **The window measured the wrong things.** "10 clients in 60 days" treats client count as the goal. The real Day 60 question should have been: "Do we have a model that produces clients repeatably?" Answer: yes, just barely, with working product proof.
2. **The performance anchor is the sales lever.** Kish capturing 10+ in 2-3 days changes every future restaurant pitch.
3. **The Persian/Iranian community is a defined go-to-market segment.** Heny + Kish + Mozhgan + Hadi's own roots. Coherent channel, not yet operationalised.
4. **Trust > Value > Price.** Confirmed across the window. Kish stated it explicitly.
5. **Bandwidth is the binding constraint, not pipeline.** Half the misses are sequencing inside one working week, not pipeline misses.
6. **Photography is in a recoverable position with focus.** Brand foundation in flight. CTR is the lever. Content cadence has to restart. If those three land in Window 2, photography returns. Otherwise decay continues.
