## The Question

Four agency deals have softened or stalled after a verbal yes in the room. Randeszvous, Maria, Kish, Ace. The yes was real in every case. The signature did not follow. We need to understand why, name the single biggest leak, and fix it for the next 10 prospects.

This is not a follow-up problem. We followed up. This is a deeper problem.

---

## The Single Biggest Leak

**The structural cause is the time gap between the verbal yes and the signature.**

Every other observation is downstream of this. The verbal yes is a high-energy emotional moment with Hadi present, anchored by social pressure, novelty, and trust built in person. The signature is a low-energy private moment in front of a PDF, with a partner asking questions, a cash flow spreadsheet open, or just buyer's remorse at 11pm. The gap between those two states is where the deals are dying.

Evidence:
- Kish: 7-minute reversal once someone (visible or invisible) intervened
- Ace: silent the moment the contract landed, 14 hours after the verbal yes
- Randeszvous: stalled after multiple contract revisions extended the gap to weeks
- Maria: gap was the longest of all, the deal never had a single closing moment

The Adobe e-sign verification email issue at Kish is the visible symptom, not the cause. Even if that email had landed, the 7-minute window would have produced the same reversal. The technical failure just delayed the inevitable by a different route.

---

## Is This Positioning, Pricing, Trust, or Process?

This is a **process problem**, not a positioning, pricing, or trust problem.

Defence:
- Positioning is working. We are getting verbal yeses. The angle (ROI and time, not technology) is landing. If positioning were broken, we would not be getting to the close conversation at all.
- Pricing is working. The case-study-led offer (waived setup, 50% off month 1, 24-month price lock) is compelling enough that owners say yes in the room. Nobody has counter-offered or pushed back on price during the verbal close.
- Trust is mostly working. Hadi is doing the closing himself. His presence is the trust mechanism. The trust holds in the room. It breaks alone.
- Process is failing. We are letting the buyer leave the closing moment without the signature, then trying to reconstruct that moment over WhatsApp and email. That reconstruction never works.

A pricing or positioning problem would show up earlier in the funnel: low reply rates on cold outreach, no second meetings, price objections during demos. None of those signals are present. The leak is at the last step, every time.

---

## Should the Contract Itself Be Reviewed?

A targeted review, yes. A full rewrite, no.

Three clauses worth reconsidering:

1. **24-month price lock language.** Designed to protect us from renegotiation, but reads to a small business owner as a long commitment. The contract is monthly rolling per Section 5.1, but the 24-month language in the loyalty discount section contradicts that reading. Recommend: rephrase as "the introductory discount structure applies for the first 24 months of continuous service" with no separate price-lock clause. The price-lock is a we-benefit clause that adds friction without adding value to the client.

2. **Case study clause.** For case-study clients, the clause grants permission for "business name, logo, and a description of the services delivered" to appear in marketing. This is the part that may scare them at the kitchen table. Recommend: add language that the client approves the case study before publication AND can withdraw permission at any time with 30 days notice. Two of the four cold deals were case-study tier. Coincidence is possible. Worth testing.

3. **7-day trial wording.** Currently triggers on go-live date. Owners may misread this as "I owe £797 if I don't cancel in 7 days from signature". Recommend: bold and clarify in the contract preamble that the trial clock starts only when the system is live, NOT when the contract is signed.

These edits are minor. None are urgent. Do not block the next 10 closes on them. Tighten in the next contract revision cycle.

## Three Protocol Changes With Highest Probability of Fixing This

These are concrete. They map to specific behaviours, not principles.

**1. Sign in the room, on the iPad, with the Apple Pencil. No exceptions for in-person closes.**

Four templates pre-loaded. Adjust live. Hand it over. Watch the signature. AirDrop the signed copy. The verbal yes and the signature happen in the same emotional moment.

This is the most important change. The full protocol is documented in `projects/agency/12-05-26/contract-signing-protocol.md`. Implementation starts with the next closing meeting.

**2. Ask the phantom decision-maker question before pricing.**

> "Is there anyone else who needs to weigh in on this decision before we move forward?"

If the answer is yes, that person joins. We do not close without them. Three of the four cold deals had a phantom decision-maker we never met. This question makes them visible.

**3. Drop Adobe e-sign for remote contracts. Switch to HelloSign or PandaDoc.**

Adobe's email verification step is a documented failure mode (Kish screenshot). For the remote scenarios where in-room signing is not possible, we need a tool with cleaner UX and reliable delivery. HelloSign or PandaDoc are both better. This is a 2-hour switchover.

## Kill Condition for This Diagnostic

This analysis is valid if:
- The next 3 deals that reach verbal yes close cleanly using the new protocol
- The protocol changes feel natural inside meetings, not forced

This analysis is wrong if:
- The next 3 deals also stall despite in-room signing protocol
- Owners react badly to being asked to sign in the moment, suggesting it is too aggressive for the cultural context

If the second scenario plays out, the next move is not "soften the protocol". It is to re-examine whether the pricing itself feels too high once seen in writing. We test the protocol fix first because it is the cheaper hypothesis.

A signal to watch over the next 14 days: the percentage of verbal yeses that convert to signatures within 24 hours. Current baseline based on the four cold deals: 0%. Target: 60% or higher. Measure after meeting 5 under the new protocol.
