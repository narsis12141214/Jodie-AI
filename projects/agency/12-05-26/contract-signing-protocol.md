# Contract Signing Protocol — In-Room and Remote

**Status:** Active, effective 12 May 2026
**Owner:** Hadi
**Applies to:** All agency closing meetings, in-person or video

This protocol replaces the previous "verbal yes then send contract" workflow which has produced four soft deals in recent weeks (Randeszvous, Maria, Kish, Ace). The gap between verbal yes and signature is where deals are dying. This protocol closes that gap.

---

## Core Principle

The signature happens in the same emotional moment as the yes. Not hours later. Not the next morning. The verbal yes is fragile and degrades quickly once the buyer is alone with a PDF.

If you cannot sign in the moment, you set a specific return time within 24 hours, in writing, before you leave.

## During the Meeting

### 1. The pre-close diagnostic question

Before any package recommendation or pricing conversation, ask:

> "Is there anyone else who needs to weigh in on this decision before we move forward?"

This is mandatory. Three of the four cold deals had a phantom decision-maker. If the answer is yes, that second person joins this call or the next one. We do not close without them in the room.

### 2. Package recommendation

After understanding the business, recommend ONE package. Not three options. The one that fits.

Frame the other tiers as upgrade paths for later. The choice in the room is yes or no to the recommendation, not "which of these three do I want".

### 3. The close question

Once they have agreed in principle, ask:

> "Do you want me to set this up so you're live within 10 days?"

If yes, move directly to signing. Do not pause. Do not say "great, I'll send you the contract tomorrow". That sentence has cost us four deals.

### 4. The in-room signing flow

1. Open the matching template on the iPad
2. Adjust client name, contact details, custom terms while they watch. Narrate what you're changing.
3. Hand the iPad over. Pass the Apple Pencil.
4. They sign on screen. You sign next.
5. AirDrop or email the signed PDF to them on the spot.
6. Confirm in the moment: "Your trial begins on the go-live date. We will be in touch within 48 hours to schedule kickoff."

The iPad never leaves your immediate eyeline until the signature is captured.

### 5. If they want time to read

This is a yellow flag, not a no. Accept it without push. Then:

> "Take it home tonight. I'll come back tomorrow morning to sign — what time works for you?"

You set the return time before you leave the room. The signature is never more than 18 hours away from this conversation. If they refuse to set a return time, that is the real signal. The deal is cold.

### 6. If they request a digital version to sign remotely

Acceptable only if the above 18-hour return window cannot be agreed. Use HelloSign or PandaDoc. Do NOT use Adobe e-sign. The verification email step is a documented friction point that contributed to the Kish loss.

After sending the remote contract:
- 24-hour silent window
- Day 2: one warm WhatsApp text confirming the contract landed
- Day 4: voice note from Hadi, no chase tone
- Day 7: one final message, then move to "stalled" status

## What This Protocol Replaces

- "I'll send you the contract tomorrow" — banned phrase, replaced by in-room signing or 18-hour return
- Adobe e-sign for in-room scenarios — replaced by direct PDF signature on iPad
- Multi-day back-and-forth on contract amendments — handled live on the iPad
- Zizi handling contract follow-up — closing communication after a verbal yes is Hadi-only. Zizi resumes after signature.

## What This Protocol Does Not Cover

- Cold outreach replies — Outreach Strategist owns that flow
- Photography enquiries — separate brand, separate close protocol
- Renewals and upsells — to be defined after first 5 clients are live

## Review Triggers

This protocol is reviewed and updated when:
- The next 5 contract attempts have closed cleanly: confirms it works
- 2 of the next 5 stall after a verbal yes: protocol is failing, escalate to Strategist
- A specific step is creating friction in the room: amend immediately, log to memory/quality-log.md

## Risk Acknowledged

This protocol increases the social pressure inside the closing meeting. Some prospects will react badly to being asked for a signature in the moment. That is acceptable. We would rather lose a deal that was never going to close than carry it for two weeks in a stall and lose it anyway. A fast no is more valuable than a slow maybe.
