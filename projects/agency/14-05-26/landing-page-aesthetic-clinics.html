What the AI does. And what it does not.

Aesthetic practice is regulated. Click is built to respect that.

The AI does:

  • Answer every inbound call 24/7
  • Book consultations into your software
  • Send confirmations, reminders, and rebooking requests
  • Capture enquiry details and route to your team
  • Run automated recall sequences for follow-up appointments

The AI does not:

  • Provide medical advice or treatment recommendations
  • Make clinical claims about outcomes
  • Promise prescribing decisions
  • Replace the prescriber consultation

Botulinum toxin is a Prescription-Only Medicine. Only registered prescribers (doctors, dentists, independent-prescribing nurses, pharmacists) may prescribe it. The AI's role ends at booking the consultation. The prescriber's role begins there. We build the agent so the line is visible to every caller.

Common questions from clinic owners and practice managers.

Will the AI provide medical advice or clinical recommendations?

No. The agent is trained to handle administrative questions and booking. Anything clinical (treatment suitability, dosing, post-procedure concerns) is routed to your prescriber immediately. We script the regulatory boundaries into the agent.

Is patient data handled compliantly?

Yes. We are UK GDPR aligned. Call recordings stored on UK-based servers. Data Processing Agreement signed before any patient data is collected. Clinic-controlled retention policies.

Will it integrate with Pabau / Phorest Medical / ANS / Consentz?

Yes for all four, plus Clinicminds. Bookings made by the voice agent sync straight into your existing system.

Can the AI handle treatment-specific questions (e.g., "Is Botox right for me?")

The agent explains general treatment information (what Botox is, what fillers are, what a consultation involves) but does not assess suitability or recommend specific treatments. Those conversations route to your prescriber during the in-person consultation.

What about prescriber rules and face-to-face consultations?

The agent books the consultation. It does not pretend to replace it. Per NMC requirements from 1 June 2025, all prescribers must conduct face-to-face consultations before prescribing injectables. The agent's job ends when the consultation is scheduled.

How does the agent handle aesthetic emergencies or complications?

The agent identifies complication keywords (swelling, vascular events, pain post-treatment, allergic reaction) and routes the caller immediately to your designated emergency line or on-call clinician. You define the protocol during onboarding.

Can callers stay anonymous if they want to?

Yes. Aesthetic enquiries are often discreet. The agent can take an enquiry, send relevant information by WhatsApp/SMS/email, and only request identifying details when the caller is ready to book.

What happens if patients don't like talking to an AI?

The agent introduces itself as your clinic's "AI assistant" and offers to transfer to a human at any point. We track patient sentiment in the first 90 days and adjust if callers consistently push back.

Can I cancel?

30 days written notice, monthly rolling. We deactivate the system and hand over all patient communication templates at no extra charge.
