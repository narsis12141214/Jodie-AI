## QUESTION 4, Positioning Notes Specific to Aesthetics

### What the aesthetics clinic owner cares about that the salon owner does not

1. **High-value individual bookings.** A single missed Botox enquiry is worth £150 to £350 in immediate booking and £2,950 plus in annual LTV. A missed salon booking is worth £30 to £60. The mathematics of urgency are different. Aesthetics owners will pay more for a system that protects high-value bookings.
2. **Patient confidentiality.** Aesthetics buyers are often discreet about treatments. Calls from work, calls hidden from partners, calls outside business hours. The owner cares that the system can take a private enquiry without exposing patient identity inappropriately.
3. **Clinical risk and reputation.** A mishandled aesthetic enquiry can lead to a complaint, a regulator referral, an insurance issue. Owners want a system that does not make clinical claims, does not promise outcomes, and does not provide medical advice.
4. **Prescriber compliance.** Aesthetics owners know that injectables require face-to-face consultation. They want an AI that books the consultation, not one that pretends to replace it.
5. **Integration with the real software they use.** A salon might run on a spreadsheet. An aesthetics clinic runs on Pabau or Phorest Medical. Integration is non-negotiable.

### Regulatory and clinical risk language that must appear

The page should explicitly state:

- The AI books consultations, it does not provide them.
- The AI does not give medical advice or recommend treatments.
- The AI does not collect or store sensitive medical data on the call.
- The AI hands over to a registered prescriber for any consultation involving Prescription-Only Medicines.
- GDPR-aligned data handling and clinic-controlled call recording policy.

These statements protect the clinic, position Click as the responsible operator, and remove a common buyer objection in the first scroll.

### Integration priorities

Order of priority for named integrations in body copy:

1. **Pabau:** largest UK aesthetics install base.
2. **Phorest Medical:** second largest, particularly for nurse-led clinics.
3. **Aesthetic Nurse Software:** nurse-prescriber niche.
4. **Consentz:** UK-built, doctor-led clinics.
5. **Clinicminds:** secondary, EMR-focused.

Generic integrations to mention as fallbacks: Google Calendar, Outlook, WhatsApp, SMS via Twilio.

### How the AI should handle pre-treatment consultation requirements

The aesthetics buyer journey is:

1. Enquiry call or DM.
2. AI books a free or low-deposit in-person consultation.
3. Prescriber assesses face-to-face.
4. Prescription issued, treatment booked.
5. Treatment delivered.
6. Follow-up and recall.

The AI's job is steps 1, 2, and 6. Steps 3, 4, 5 are the practitioner's. The page must say this clearly. Buyers will trust the system more when it knows where the line is.
