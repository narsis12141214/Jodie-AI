## SUMMARY (3 sentences)

The phrase "service businesses" is the right audience descriptor for Click's homepage H1: it is specific enough to filter out e-commerce and SaaS noise, broad enough to cover restaurants, dental, fitness and aesthetics, and aligns with how the segment pages already speak. The homepage should be brand-led with a light category anchor (Option B), because the segment pages already carry the long-tail SEO load and a brand homepage converts direct, brand-search and outreach traffic far better than a keyword-stuffed homepage. Hadi's draft headline is structurally correct but the word "Operations" weakens the outcome promise; the recommended H1 is **"Never Miss Another Booking. Done-for-you AI for UK Service Businesses."**

### JUDGEMENT — Recommended descriptor

**"Service businesses"** is the strongest choice for the homepage H1.

Reasoning:
1. Click's four segment pages (restaurants/cafes, dental, fitness, aesthetic clinics) all describe service businesses. The homepage descriptor should be the parent category, not a synonym of one of the children.
2. "Small businesses" pulls in e-commerce, SaaS and retail traffic that will never convert. Filtering at the H1 level saves outreach and ad spend later.
3. "SMEs" is UK-correct but technical and bloodless. It reads HMRC, not "we get you."
4. "Service businesses" is plain English, mirrors the buyer's self-description (a restaurant owner calls themselves a restaurant, but accepts "service business" as the bucket they sit in), and is observably unclaimed in UK competitor copy.
5. It directly cues the core problem Click solves: missed calls, slow follow-up, booking gaps — all symptoms unique to service-led, booking-led businesses.

## QUESTION 2 — BRAND-LED VS SEO-DISCOVERABLE HOMEPAGE

### The strategic position

Click already has four segment landing pages built and indexed (restaurants/cafes, dental, fitness, aesthetic clinics). These pages target the long-tail commercial queries — "AI receptionist for dental practices UK", "AI booking system for restaurants London", and so on. They will carry the SEO load.

The homepage receives three traffic types:
- **Direct** — typed clickaiagency.com (outreach replies, business cards, podcast mentions)
- **Brand search** — "click ai agency", "clickaiagency"
- **Referral / social** — Instagram bio link, LinkedIn, email signatures

None of these visitors arrive because of a homepage SEO keyword. They arrive because the brand was mentioned to them. The homepage's job is to convert them, not to compete with the segment pages.

### FACT — SEO best practice on cannibalisation

According to Boomcycle and SEOmonitor, homepage cannibalisation happens when a broad-brand homepage and a specific service page compete for the same query, with the homepage usually winning the rank but losing the conversion because the page is less specific (boomcycle.com, seomonitor.com). Aggressive multi-keyword homepages create exactly this problem when child pages exist.

Backlinko and W3era both state that exact-keyword H1s show no meaningful ranking lift in 2026, and that "clarity beats keywords" for homepage H1s (backlinko.com, w3era.com). H1 keyword stuffing is a dead lever.

### JUDGEMENT — Recommended approach

**Option B: Light category-keyword anchor.**

Not pure brand-led (Option A) because the homepage should still tell Google in one phrase what the business is. "Done-for-you AI for UK Service Businesses" gives Google enough to bucket the page as "AI agency UK" without competing with "AI receptionist for restaurants UK" (owned by the restaurants page).

Not aggressive (Option C) because each segment page is the optimised landing pad for vertical-specific queries. A homepage that tries to rank for "AI receptionist for dentists" will steal authority from the dental page and dilute conversion.

The right structure:
- **H1:** outcome promise + light category anchor ("Done-for-you AI for UK Service Businesses")
- **H2 / subhead:** brand mechanism and positioning
- **Body:** four segment cards each linking down to the dedicated landing page
- **Internal links:** every segment page links back to homepage with brand anchor; homepage links out to segment pages with exact-match anchor text ("AI for restaurants and cafes", "AI for dental practices", etc.)

This is the same pattern Hello AI uses on usehello.ai (parent "Healthcare AI Voice Infrastructure" + specialty child pages for dental, dermatology, ophthalmology). It is also how Slang AI and Loman AI structure their multi-vertical expansion.

### JUDGEMENT — What wins

Three patterns dominate the strongest competitors (Slang, Hello, ViveoAI):
1. **Outcome-led H1** ("Never Miss a Patient Call Again") with a vertical or category anchor in the subhead.
2. **Category-led H1 with a specific subhead outcome** ("Healthcare AI Voice Infrastructure" + "Answer every patient call. Capture after-hours revenue.").
3. **Brand-as-role H1** ("The AI Superhost for Restaurants") — strong but only works when one vertical owns the brand.

The weakest H1s are pure category labels (MQLFlow, Opscaling, Ronins) — they don't differentiate, don't promise an outcome, and don't filter the audience.

UK competitors are noticeably 12–18 months behind US ones on H1 craft. Most UK AI agency homepages still read as 2023 category labels. This is an opportunity.

### Test against Hadi's draft

Hadi's draft: *"Never Miss Another Booking. Done-for-you AI Operations for UK Service Businesses."*

What works:
- Outcome-led primary clause matches the strongest US pattern (ViveoAI, Bland, Smith.ai).
- "Done-for-you" is unclaimed by competitors and differentiates from DIY tools.
- "UK Service Businesses" filters the right audience.

What weakens it:
- "Operations" is abstract. It softens the outcome. Restaurants and clinics don't buy "operations"; they buy "more bookings", "fewer missed calls", "more replies". The first clause already named the outcome; the second clause should name the deliverable plainly.

## FINAL RECOMMENDATION

### H1 (use this)

**Never Miss Another Booking. Done-for-you AI for UK Service Businesses.**

Defence:
The first sentence is the outcome — short, concrete, mirrors the dominant US pattern (Bland, Smith.ai, ViveoAI, RingCentral) which is observably the highest-converting AI receptionist headline structure. The second sentence is the category anchor — "Done-for-you AI" differentiates from DIY tools (the real competition in UK), and "UK Service Businesses" filters the audience without going as narrow as a single vertical (which would conflict with the segment pages). Dropping "Operations" tightens the promise and removes the only abstract word.

This H1 will not compete with the segment pages (which target "AI receptionist for restaurants UK" and similar), and will rank cleanly for branded queries and the broad bucket "done-for-you AI UK".

### Page Title (60 chars max)

**Click AI Agency: Done-for-you AI for UK Service Businesses** (58 chars)

### Meta Description (155 chars max)

**We build AI receptionists, follow-up systems and content engines for UK restaurants, clinics and salons. Never miss another booking.** (138 chars)

### H2 (positioning expansion)

**One system that answers the phone, follows up the leads and runs your content. Without you lifting a finger.**

### First paragraph hook (~50 words)

Every missed call is a booking you paid to chase and lost at the door. Click installs the AI that answers, books and follows up for your restaurant, clinic, salon or studio. We build it, we run it, you keep the bookings. One monthly fee. No hiring. No software to learn.
