The Problem

Four things costing your practice money every single week

These are not hypotheticals. These are the patterns we see in every private dental practice we work with. They happen because your team is treating patients, not because they are doing anything wrong.

📞

Calls missed while the team is busy

Your receptionist is on a call. Another patient walks up to the desk. The phone rings again. Nobody picks up. That patient does not leave a voicemail. They Google the next practice on the list and book there. You will never see the invoice for that appointment.

Each missed call: £150 to £400 lost
🪑

No-shows that leave chairs empty

A patient was booked for a 45-minute appointment. They forgot. The reminder was not sent because the receptionist assumed the other shift would handle it. The chair sat empty. The dentist was paid regardless. That slot could have been filled from the waitlist.

10 no-shows/week = £3,000+/month lost
🔀

Wrong bookings from shift handovers

A patient was booked with the wrong practitioner because the receptionist did not check the notes from the last visit. The patient arrives, the mistake surfaces at the desk, and the experience that took months to build takes thirty seconds to damage.

Shift handover is where errors live
🚪

Staff turnover that resets everything

It takes three months to properly train a new receptionist on your treatments, practitioners, and systems. By month four, the good ones leave for somewhere that pays more. You are stuck in a loop you can see clearly but cannot get out of.

Average receptionist tenure: 14 months
