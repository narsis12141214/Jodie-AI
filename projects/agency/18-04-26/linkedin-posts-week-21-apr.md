## POST 2 — Monday 21 April

**Pillar:** Problem / Education
**Hook mechanic:** Curiosity gap
**Target:** Dental practice owners

Nobody tracks this number, but it is costing dental practices more than rent.

It is not marketing spend. It is not equipment. It is not even staff wages.

It is the patients who call and nobody picks up.

Most practices miss between 15 and 30 calls a week. Not because the team is lazy. Because the receptionist is already on a call, or with a patient at the desk, or on lunch. The phone rings. Nobody answers. The patient calls the next practice on Google.

Each missed call is worth £150 to £400 in treatment revenue. Multiply that by 20 calls a week and the number gets uncomfortable fast.

We built a system for a London clinic that answers every call automatically. Knows the treatments. Books the appointment. Sends the confirmation. No hold time. No voicemail. No lost patients.

Their no-show rate dropped by more than half in the first month. Not because of a new marketing strategy. Because patients were actually getting through.

If you run a practice, I can show you what that system looks like for yours.

When I showed him the demo, he listened for about 10 minutes. Then he said: "When can we start?"
