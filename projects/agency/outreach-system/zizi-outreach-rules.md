# Zizi — Outreach Message Rules
## Click AI Agency — Updated 9 April 2026

These rules apply to every message you send. Generated openers and manual messages follow the same rules. No exceptions.

---

## Step 1 Openers — What to Check Before Sending

The AI generates your openers. Before you send any opener, read it and check these three things:

**1. No compliments**
Remove any line that says how you feel about what they do.

Remove: "Love that you...", "Great that you...", "Impressive that...", "Rare to see...", "Good to see...", "Genuinely impressive"

Replace with: a plain observation of what you noticed. State the fact, not your feeling about it.

Wrong: "Love that you lead with natural enhancements, that's a trust-first positioning."
Right: "Saw you lead with natural enhancements across five locations."

**2. No em dashes**
Em dashes look like this: —
Never use them in any message. Use a comma, colon, or full stop instead.

Wrong: "We help restaurants — and cafes — automate their content."
Right: "We help restaurants and cafes automate their content."

**3. End with the permission question**
Every opener must end with: "Would this be the best place to ask you a quick question?"
Do not change this line.

---

## Step 2 Messages — What to Never Say

Once they reply and you move to Step 2, never use these phrases:

| Never say | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| "No pressure" | Signals low confidence |
| "No worries" | Pre-approves them saying no |
| "No problem at all" | Same as above |
| "Free trial" or "free" anything | Devalues the offer |
| "Just checking in" | Signals you are not confident |
| "Whenever you're ready" | Gives permission to delay forever |

---

## Step 2 Formula

Every Step 2 message follows this structure:

1. One line on what we do and what problem it solves for their specific business type
2. One line on how to see it: "Can I send you a quick screen recording of how it works?"

Keep it short. Two to three lines maximum. End with a question.

---

## When They Misunderstand

Sometimes they think you are looking to buy from them, not sell to them. If that happens:

"Ha, appreciate that! Actually I'm on the other side of things. We help businesses like yours automate their content and client follow-up so it runs without you having to manage it manually. Would that be worth a quick look?"

---

## When They Ask a Technical Question

Do not try to answer technical questions in detail. Use this:

"It connects to whatever you are already using: booking systems, WhatsApp, email, Google reviews. No new software to learn. It is easier to show than explain. Can I send you a quick screen recording of how it works for a venue like yours?"

---

## Quick Checklist Before Every Send

- No compliments (no "love that", "great that", "impressive")
- No em dashes (—)
- No "free", "no pressure", "no worries"
- Ends with a question
- Maximum 3 lines for Step 2

If in doubt, send it to Hadi before you hit send.
