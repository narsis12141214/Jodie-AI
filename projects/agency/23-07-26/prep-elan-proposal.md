## Purpose

Win Elan Cafe's signature on the cake-ordering system (Ella voice agent + custom cake designer page + Stripe payments + Click Pro order management) for the London branch, at £5,000 implementation + £500/month, on a 12-month partnership agreement, in time for a 1 August go-live.

The document must do three jobs:
1. Answer their real question — reliability, uptime, long-term support — before they ask it.
2. Arm Ali Bidarbakht to defend the decision internally (marketing and accounting are both intervening; the proposal is his weapon, so it must be forwardable, plain, and numbers-clean).
3. Define per-branch deployment scope so the "reuse London's page for the USA branch" assumption dies quietly on paper, while keeping the international rollout warm and incentivised.

## Locked foundation pieces that apply

- Positioning: "Done-for-you AI Operations Partner" — sell the team and the service depth, not the tool (decisions.md 2026-05-13).
- Setup and monthly must read as visibly different numbers (decisions.md 2026-05-13, permanent principle). Here: £5,000 vs £500.

- Payment milestones tied to objective events, never satisfaction conditions (this session, 23 July).

## Deal facts (confirmed by Hadi, 23 July)

- Champion: Ali Bidarbakht, Head of Pastry. Second decision-maker: business partner (name TBC), demo pending.
- Voice agent name: Ella.
- Target go-live: 1 August 2026.
- Scope v1: Ella answers the cake-ordering line only. Standard cakes: takes cake choice, name, contact details, sends Stripe payment link. Custom cakes: sends link to the bespoke cake designer page (built, demoed, approved). Completed orders land in Click Pro.
- Volume basis: ~10 calls/day ≈ 300 calls ≈ ~900-1,000 minutes/month.
- Running costs borne by Click: ElevenLabs, voice agent stack, Click Desk/Click Pro.

## Commercial structure (for green-light)

- Implementation: £5,000. £2,500 on agreement, £2,500 due 30 days after go-live (date-based, not satisfaction-based).
- Service: £500/month, 1,000 minutes included, billed from go-live. Rate locked for the 12-month term. Usage above 1,000 minutes flagged in the monthly report and next tier agreed together, no surprise billing.
- Term: 12-month partnership agreement, quarterly service reviews. First quarterly review explicitly includes the multi-branch expansion conversation.
- Expansion incentive: any additional Elan branch signed within 12 months receives London's implementation rate, service billed per branch.
- IP/licence: platform, page design and system remain Click AI Agency property, licensed to Elan per site under the service agreement.
- Support scope: system monitoring; monthly service report (calls answered, orders processed, revenue through Stripe); menu/product updates included with 48-hour turnaround (allocation cap TBC by Hadi); named support channel, same-business-day response; payments and card security handled by Stripe.
- Roadmap section: phase 2 possibilities — beyond cake orders, additional branches, loyalty via Click Pro.

## What this piece must NOT do

- No "free", no "bonus", no "discount" framing. The £5,000 is the flagship rate, stated straight.
- No conditional payment language ("if happy", "if satisfied").
- No open-ended or unlimited support promises. Everything scoped, everything named.
- No silence on branch reuse. Per-branch deployment must be defined, positively framed as rollout speed.
- No uptime percentages we cannot evidence. Reliability expressed as monitoring + response commitments + Stripe/ElevenLabs infrastructure, not invented SLA numbers.
- No jargon, no em dashes, no corporate warm-up. Plain language a Head of Pastry forwards without translating.

## What good looks like

Ali reads it in five minutes, feels the reliability question was answered before he asked it, forwards it internally without edits, and walks into the partner demo with the commercial decision already made. The expansion door is visibly open and visibly priced.
