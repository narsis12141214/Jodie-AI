## Executive Summary

1. The query restaurant owners actually type is "AI phone answering for restaurants" or "AI voice agent for restaurants" — not "AI operations partner". Loman and Slang both rank with literal versions of this phrase in H1.
2. Dental practice owners search "AI receptionist for dental practices UK" — Linda AI, DentaAI, Arini, and VoiceFleet all rank with near-identical literal H1s. The pattern is locked: lead with "AI Receptionist for [segment]".
3. Beauty and fitness search behaviour skews toward "AI booking system" and "AI receptionist" with strong overlap on "24/7" and "never miss a call" modifiers.
4. UK-specific intent modifiers (UK, London) materially shift the SERP. Linda AI ranks first in UK dental because its title carries "UK & Ireland". This is a low-cost lever Click can copy.
5. The single highest-value SEO move is decoupling H1 (search query) from H2 (positioning). Body copy carries the "Done-for-you AI Operations Partner" framing safely. Headlines must match what people type.

## PART 2 — Competitor Headline Pattern Analysis

The structural pattern that wins both restaurant and dental SERPs is identical:

```
[Number/Modifier] AI [Phone | Receptionist | Voice Agent] [for] [Segment plural]
```

Examples in market:

- "24/7 AI Phone Answering for Restaurants" (Loman)
- "AI Receptionist for Dental Clinics" (Linda AI)
- "Human-like AI Phone Receptionist Specifically for UK Dental Practices" (DentaAI)
- "AI Receptionist for Dental Clinics in London" (VoiceFleet)
- "The AI Front Desk for Dental Groups. Zero missed calls, 24/7." (Arini)

**What we learn:**

- Every winning H1 contains the segment noun (restaurants, dental, salons, gyms).
- Every winning H1 contains the product noun (AI receptionist, AI phone answering, AI voice agent).
- Geographic qualifiers (UK, London) lift relevance for local SERPs but are absent from US competitors. This is Click's edge.
- Outcome qualifiers (24/7, zero missed calls, never miss a call) appear in H2 or sub-headline more often than H1.
- Brand positioning language (superhost, front desk, operations partner) appears below H1, never as H1.

**Pattern application for Click:** lead H1 with literal search query, push positioning to H2, carry the "Done-for-you AI Operations Partner" framing through body copy and section headers.

## PART 4 — Recommendations

### Single best H1 for the new Click Voice page

> **AI Phone Answering for UK Restaurants**

Reasoning: this exact phrasing matches the highest-intent UK commercial query in this segment (top 5 ranking competitors all use minor variants), carries the "UK" qualifier most US competitors lack, and reads naturally above a Click Voice product hero. It does not need "Click Voice" in the H1 because Click Voice is the product name, not the search query.

### SEO and positioning balance, one paragraph

Headlines and page titles must use the literal words the buyer types into Google. Body copy is where the buyer learns who you are. The fix is structural: H1 carries the keyword ("AI Receptionist for UK Dental Practices"), H2 introduces the positioning ("Done-for-you AI Operations Partner for dental clinics"), and the rest of the page expands the partner framing through proof, process, and team. SEO bots read H1 and meta. Humans read H2 and body. Both win when the H1 is literal and the H2 is differentiated.

### Risks

1. **Category framing risk:** "AI Receptionist" and "AI Voice Agent" are now crowded categories. Click's edge is the bundle and the human team. The risk is that traffic lands, sees a product page, and assumes Click is another SaaS tool. Mitigation: body copy must establish the "we run this for you" framing within the first 200 words of every page.
2. **Long-tail vs short-tail trade-off:** UK volumes on these queries are modest (low hundreds per month per term). Click should not expect SEO alone to drive pipeline. Mitigation: pair landing pages with direct outreach and Google Business Profile / Google Maps optimisation for "AI receptionist London" later.
3. **Positioning erosion risk:** if every H1 leads with "AI Receptionist", Click risks looking like Linda AI or DentaAI in the SERP. Mitigation: meta titles must include "Click AI Agency" or "for UK [segment]" to differentiate at the click-through stage, and the H2 must introduce the partner framing on every page.
4. **Click Voice cannibalising Click+ risk:** an entry tier at £290 could anchor every conversation downward. Mitigation: page architecture upsells naturally. Click Voice is the door. Click+ is the room.
