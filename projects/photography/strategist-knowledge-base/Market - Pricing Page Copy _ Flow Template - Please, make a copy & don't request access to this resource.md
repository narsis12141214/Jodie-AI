**IMPORTANT NOTE about plagiarism and paraphrasing other people’s copy ↓**

**First off, a friendly reminder, that copying another photographer's website content (even if you change the words around or paraphrase) is still plagiarism if you don't credit them.** A quick google search of "if you paraphrase is it plagiarism?" has dozens of fact-checked legal articles to confirm this. 

**Plagiarism is a violation of U.S. copyright law** (and the laws of most other countries) and regardless of where you live, it can lead to getting your website taken down by your host (if someone files a [DMCA](https://www.dmca.com/FAQ/Someone-has-stolen-my-website-content#:~:text=Collect%20proof%20of%20copyright%20infringement,content%2C%20and%20document%20website%20URLs.) takedown), or being sued for damages, or even fined. 

**Plagiarism is not just serious cases of copying whole paragraphs or pages**—it also includes copying headlines, sentences and design solutions. 

As cliche as it is, **the key to a GREAT website is to 100% just be *yourself.*** 

You have everything you need within you, and all of the resources at your fingertips to write powerful and completely *unique* website copy, I promise. 

You don’t need to be a “second best” version of someone else. **You don’t need to “sound professional” or use cliche buzzwords. Just talk how you do normally\!**

Something else I need to say because I’ve seen too many photographers get steered the wrong direction here. 

**Copying anything from my website is the absolute worst thing you can do for your business.** 

I’m being blunt here intentionally, because many THRIVE members have done this thinking it will help them succeed, get better conversion rates, or get more inquiries… and then get frustrated and confused when their inquiry numbers go DOWN after doing this. 

Why does this happen? Because I’m an educator and my website is public, it’s not just THRIVE members who will take too close of inspiration from my website—it’s thousands of photographers in almost every competitive market around the world. This means that versions and paraphrased versions of my website copy, my headlines, my design layouts—yes, even my cute designed icons are *EVERYWHERE*. 

Which means… **anything that’s on my website is already commonplace, overused, and generic by default.** 

So if you take a headline you like or a design solution you like from my website… you’re making your website look exactly like thousands of other photographers websites—which instantly makes yours not unique… but generic and stuck in the “sea of sameness.” This leads to lower inquiries and price shopping—and makes your brand feel forgettable which is the exact opposite of what I want for you.

I promise you there are hundreds of better ways to write your website than using any version of my headlines, my design layouts, my copy, etc. I’ve also changed my own website copy a dozen times in the last 5 years and it still converts—so I promise you, the way I’ve written mine is nothing magical. What will be magical is you being you. 

**Bottom line:** you will get SO much further so much faster intentionally creating something different and better than what I’m doing (and what anyone else is doing) and my mission in creating THRIVE is to give you ALL of the tools you need to do that.

# My top tips to write your own, completely unique website copy 

This will successfully help you stand out, get picked OVER your competitors, and be able to successfully charge higher rates:

* **Utilize the BRAND section of THRIVE to get clarity & ideas first**—this will help you identify your specialty, what makes you unique, and who your clients really are. 

  These are the most helpful exercises in the BRAND section to help with your website copy. **You will have more than enough copy ideas to work with if you do these first\!** 

  *Note: These are all in the Creating Your Irresistible Brand fillable workbook (linked under every single one of the BRAND videos).*  
  * “4 lists exercise”   
  * Brainstorm & narrow down your speciality  
  * Brainstorm & narrowing down your differentiators  
  * Develop your ICA(s)  
  * Write your Brand Message & Story  
  * **Take the time to INTERVIEW 2-3 of your best past clients.** There’s a guide for this in the “Discovering Your Target Market” video in the BRAND section of THRIVE that has an email template and the questions to ask them in an interview. Make sure you record it & get it transcribed. ([https://revoldiv.com/](https://revoldiv.com/) transcribes up to 2-hour audio files for free)  
* **If you struggle to write, try talking it out verbally and have it transcribed**—partner up with another photographer or a good friend and have them ask you the questions in the THRIVE brand exercises and get your audio transcribed or just talk to yourself and talk through the BRAND section exercises in a voice message and get it transcribed. Try these tools:  
  * [https://revoldiv.com/](https://revoldiv.com/) transcribes up to 2-hour audio files for free  
  * [https://www.monologue.to/](https://www.monologue.to/) is $100 a year for unlimited speech-to-text, and it auto formats & edits for you  
  * [https://wisprflow.ai/](https://wisprflow.ai/) is $12 a month for for unlimited speech-to-text, and it auto formats & edits for you  
* **Do not look at any other photographers websites** (including mine) when you are working your branding exercises or writing your website copy. It’s incredibly difficult to “forget” other people’s words, and this almost always results in accidental plagiarism, which is very hard to fix.   
  * **If you are looking for design inspiration, look at websites in completely different industries** (ex. Luxury brands, outdoor brands, travel & adventure brands, spa, wellness, fitness, health brands, luxury personal service brands, or any brands that are related to your ideal client avatars' hobbies & interests).  
* **Record 2-3 consultation calls (with permission) and get them transcribed** ([https://revoldiv.com/](https://revoldiv.com/) transcribes up to 2-hour audio files for free). Look at what YOU say, and look at what your clients say and highlight any similar concepts, ideas, and phrases. Use how you naturally talk to your clients to write your website copy (it needs to sound like YOU).   
* **Analyze your client reviews carefully and use their quotes everywhere in your website copy**—Real client quotes will sell you better than you can ever sell yourself. You will also notice patterns of themes they say are the biggest benefits they got from working with you.   
* **When writing your copy, focus your inspiration ONLY on your Ideal Client Avatars**—you are writing ONLY to them on your website, write their “name” at the top of a blank page (journal or google doc), and just start talking to them. What do THEY need to hear? Tune everyone else out—your website isn’t for a huge group of people. Your website isn’t for other photographers, who cares what they think. Just focus on talking 1 on 1 to your dream clients ONLY.   
* **Remember your website is your “elevator pitch” for your business**. Don’t waste too much of your website copy convincing people to elope or have the wedding day they want—spend MORE of your time making your website copy a persuasive argument about why they should choose YOU as their photographer. The more you can point out how you approach or do things differently from your competitors, the better. The more you can sound UNLIKE the other photographers in your market, the better.   
* **Avoid using overused words & phrases or feeling pressure to add fancy-sounding “buzzwords” in your website copy.** Here’s a list of some of the most overused words and phrases on wedding & elopement photographer sites. These are so overused that they have become meaningless and cliché and I recommend avoiding them entirely if possible so that your website copy does not sound like the same generic messaging as many other photographers. Tip: Do a “Control \+ F” on your site to find them.  
  * Authentic / authentically / authenticity  
  * Timeless  
  * Intentional / intentionally   
  * Moments   
  * Memories   
  * Capture / capturing / captured   
  * Telling your story / Telling your love story / Visual storyteller  
  * Unforgettable  
  * Documentary  
  * Stress-free  
  * Intimate  
  * Meaningful  
  * Unique / uniqueness  
  * Personal / Personalized  
  * Connection / Rooted in connection  
  * Not your average photographer / Not your ordinary photographer  
  * More than just a photographer / more than just photos  
  * Wild at heart / Wild hearted / for the wild at heart   
  * For the passionately in love / for the \[hopelessly/wildly/fearlessly\] in love  
  * Adventurous / For adventurous souls / For couples who love adventure   
  * Modern romantic / For the modern romantics  
  * Capture how it felt / remember how it felt  
  * You deserve / You deserve more / you deserve it   
* **Read your website copy OUT LOUD before you finish.** Ask yourself, “Does this honestly sound like me? Would I actually SAY these things to a client on a consultation call?” If not, make it more YOU. Forget about sounding professional or what you’re “supposed to say” on your website. 

**Remember: If your website has the same generic messaging as other people in your market (i.e. other wedding or elopement photographers who serve a same or similar area), this will lead to you getting price-shopped.** 

You will see a HUGE difference in your marketing, website conversion rate, ability to charge higher rates if you can break away from what everyone else is saying on their website and be 100% YOURSELF. 

Stand out with your unique opinions, your unique approach, your unique brand voice and personality. 

I promise you ARE unique. 

**Absolutely no one else has the exact life experience, personality, energy, passion, motivations, visual eye, way of working with couples, etc. that YOU do.**

**Final VERY IMPORTANT TIP:**

* **Give yourself a lot of grace & love throughout this process 🫶** Websites are hard, introspection is hard, being yourself is hard and vulnerable. It’s not supposed to be easy, but it is worth it. 

You’ve got this\!  
Pricing Page (Public) \- Example Blocks & Flow

**Note: You do NOT need to have ALL of these blocks on your pricing page and they do NOT need to be in this exact order.** 

**The more you can make this your OWN, the better.** It’s best if you mix up this order so it flows naturally for YOU and isn’t like anyone else’s pricing page.

**The ONLY content / blocks you absolutely need to have on your pricing page are:**

- Above-the-fold block w/ clear UVP that mentions “pricing” visible without scrolling  
- Short introduction of yourself & highlighting your differentiators   
- Section(s) that explains everything that a client gains when they hire you (what’s included in ALL of your packages… i.e. your “value stack”). Make sure to include clear explanations of why each line item actually matters to YOUR CLIENTS — making sure to point out how your service is DIFFERENT from your competitors.  
- Starting price (or price range) with a clear explanation of exactly what that starting price includes (hours, travel, etc)  
- Several client testimonials  
- Several CTA buttons to your contact form (especially next to your value stack / starting pricing) and I do recommend embedding an actual contact form soon after your starting prices as well
