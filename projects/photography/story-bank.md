# Hadi Photography London — Story Bank

**Purpose:** A living archive of specific moments, stories, and details from real sessions. This is the raw material for every reel, carousel, blog post, caption, and DM. Generic content comes from generic sources. Specific content comes from here.

**Rule:** Raw beats polished. Write it like you'd tell it to a friend, not like you're writing a caption.

---

## The 5 Pillars

| # | Pillar | What it is | What it does |
|---|--------|-----------|--------------|
| 1 | **My Story** | Who I am, my vision, behind the scenes, photojournalism days, how I see light, emotional moments with clients | Builds the person-to-person connection. People buy from people. |
| 2 | **The Belief** | What the industry gets wrong. My point of view on the work, the day, the photography. | POV is the brand. This is what makes me different from every other photographer. |
| 3 | **Results / Client Wins** | Client transformations, testimonials in story form, the nervousness-to-ease moment, my own transformation | Turns browsers into buyers. Proof over claim. |
| 4 | **Education** | One technique, one business lesson, one thing I wish I'd known earlier | Positions me as authority without ever claiming it. |
| 5 | **The Human** | The struggle, the wins, the things I wouldn't usually talk about. Books I'm reading, walks, observations, thoughts | Makes people feel like they know me. This is why people fall in love with a brand. |

---

## How to use this bank

1. **Add stories as they come to mind.** Don't wait for a content session. Dump it here.
2. **Tag each story with one or more pillars.** A single story can serve multiple pillars.
3. **Raw is better than polished.** Don't write the caption. Write the moment.
4. **Every shoot, add one line.** After every session, the 60 seconds it takes to note the standout moment pays back every time you need content.
5. **Mark anything off-limits.** If a client wouldn't want a story shared publicly, flag it. We can still use the lesson without the specifics.

## Entry template

Copy this block and fill it in for each new story.

```
## [Short memorable title]
- Date: [approximate is fine]
- Clients: [names or initials]
- Location: [Greenwich / Westminster / Pergola / etc]
- Pillar(s): [1, 2, 3, 4, 5 — can be more than one]
- Emotional tone: [nervous / confident / funny / quiet / emotional / surprising]
- Usable for: [reel / carousel / blog / caption / DM]
- Permission: [public / initials only / private/internal use]

**The moment:**
2-3 sentences, raw. Not polished. Tell it like you'd tell a friend.

**The outcome:**
What happened after, if relevant. A message they sent. A print they bought. A referral. A review line.

**Quote / exact language (if any):**
If the client said something verbatim that landed, write it here word for word.
```

## Seed questions (to unlock more stories)

**For Hadi — answer these in any format. Bullet points, voice notes, half-sentences. Don't polish. I'll turn the answers into bank entries.**

### Pillar 1 — My Story
1. What's the first wedding or shoot you ever did? What went right, what went wrong?
2. What did you learn from photojournalism that changed how you photograph couples?
3. When did you realise the pre-wedding shoot was the mechanism, not an add-on?
4. What's a moment from a shoot that you still think about years later?
5. Why did you move from photojournalism to wedding photography? Was there a specific moment or realisation?

### Pillar 2 — The Belief
6. What do most wedding photographers get wrong that drives you up the wall?
7. What do couples think they're paying for vs what they're actually paying for?
8. What's a common piece of wedding photography advice you think is nonsense?
9. What's the one thing most people underestimate about their wedding day?
10. What's a trend in photography right now that you disagree with?

### Pillar 3 — Results / Client Wins
11. Which client transformation do you remember most vividly — who arrived nervous and left completely different?
12. What's a message from a client that stopped you in your tracks?
13. When has a shoot produced a photo that genuinely surprised the couple?
14. Who was the most camera-shy couple you've worked with, and what changed during the shoot?
15. What's the biggest compliment a client has ever paid you?

### Pillar 4 — Education
16. If you could teach one technique to every couple before their shoot, what would it be?
17. What's one thing you wish you'd known when you started?
18. What mistake did you make early on that taught you something important?
19. What's a piece of advice you give every couple that nobody else gives?
20. What business lesson from running a photography business could translate to any small business?

### Pillar 5 — The Human
21. What are you reading right now? Why?
22. What made you laugh this week?
23. What's a thought you've had recently about life, work, or relationships that's worth sharing?
24. What's something you observe on your walks that nobody else would notice?
25. What's something you find hard about this work that you wouldn't usually say out loud?

---

*Answer as many or as few as you want. One story per question is plenty. These become the foundation of the next 30 days of content.*
