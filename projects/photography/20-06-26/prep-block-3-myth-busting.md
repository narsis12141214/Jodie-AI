## 1. Purpose (per Maddie Mae's Home Page Copy/Flow Template)

This is the optional Myth-Busting / Objection-Handling block. Per Maddie:

> *"If your dream clients have a common misconception that they believe that holds them back from understanding the value of your offer, or a common fear/worry, dispel this myth right away, reassure them. This could also be a space to continue to highlight your differentiators and spell out exactly what makes you different from your competitors (i.e. the 'myth' or 'objection' could be something that most other photographers do/require/say that you don't agree with)."*

The block sits between the Hero (Block 1) + Emotional Hook (Block 2) and the Testimonials + About Me blocks. Its job: name the three concerns the ICA carries about photographing a London wedding, and dispel each with Hadi's actual practice and credentials. The block should make a couple think: *"He's thought about exactly the things I was worried about. He has a real answer for each."*

## 3. Hadi's real client voice input — Step 1 (today, 20 June)

Hadi's verbatim input:

> *"Most couples are concerned about their photos not being too touristy. This is something I've been hearing from my clients. They want London to be part of their story, but not touristy photos, although most of the photos are taken in touristy places. They rather focus on their relationship and have London's landmarks as a great backdrop in their picture so it resonates with them and it's a simple way to have London in their photos. The main character, the main hero, is going to be the couple, so touristy images and not being able to guide the couples in busy areas are couple's concern, usually, and, of course, the weather, so three things."*

**The three concerns to address:**
1. **Touristy aesthetic** — they want London but not the cliché tourist look. The couple should be the hero, not the landmark.
2. **Crowds / busy areas** — they don't know how a photographer will guide them through crowded spots without it feeling exposed or rushed.
3. **London weather** — unpredictable, grey, rain.

**The underlying truth the couple wants to hear:** "London is in your photographs as the supporting role. The relationship is the lead. The photographer knows how to make this happen even in the messiest conditions."

## 6. What this piece must NOT do

### Recycled imagery — DROP completely
- ❌ The corridor outside the dressing room
- ❌ The doorway before the ceremony
- ❌ The look across the room
- ❌ The laugh in the morning
- ❌ The way one of you holds the other's sleeve
- ❌ The breath in the corridor

These appeared in 3+ prior drafts. They are now clichés.

### Recycled phrasings — DROP
- ❌ "Not the parts everyone planned. The minutes nobody did." (Block 2 v1 tic)
- ❌ "Twenty years of photojournalism trained me to see..." — Block 2 already uses this credential. Block 3 must use STREET PHOTOGRAPHY as the credential, not photojournalism again.
- ❌ "What is harder to find is..." (close cousin of the X-vs-Y tic)
- ❌ The "I work quietly" line (was Block 2 Draft C, now retired)

### Block 2 echoes — AVOID
- Do not echo Block 2's "the photographs you will love most" promise
- Do not re-state "the photographs that hold what was actually there" (Block 2 closing area)
- Block 3 is the differentiator + objection-handling block. It explains HOW, not WHAT.

### Banned-word crutches
- intimate, capture, moments, authentic, story, memories, unique, personal, timeless, unforgettable, modern romantic, you deserve, capture how it felt, wild at heart, adventurous, more than just a photographer, for the passionately in love, meaningful, connection, intentional
- "documentary" is fine when accurate (it's part of Hadi's actual photographic background)

### Other
- ❌ Em dashes (absolute rule)
- ❌ Pedagogical / teaching-mode register (12 June correction)
- ❌ Generic "I know London" hand-waving — be SPECIFIC about how he handles each concern
- ❌ Naming the cliché London tourist spots (Tower Bridge etc) as the negative example — Maddie's UVP examples show specificity by NAMING what you bring instead, not by deriding alternatives

## 8. Suggested draft structure (subject to Hadi's green-light)

| Paragraph | Job |
|---|---|
| **H2** | Bold statement naming the three concerns + reframing them as not-the-problem. Carries "London wedding" SEO. |
| **Para 1** | Acknowledge what the couple knows: most wedding photographs from London are taken in the same handful of locations, in clear weather, with the building filling the frame. Brief, no derision. |
| **Para 2** | Pivot to the actual credential: STREET PHOTOGRAPHY. Specifically — not generic "20 years of photojournalism." Name what street photography teaches: observation, timing, confidence in imperfect conditions. |
| **Para 3** | Brief, specific handling of all three concerns — one line each. Touristy = couple is subject, location is supporting cast. Crowds = ten years of London, quiet pockets exist near every busy spot. Weather = grey light and wet stone are some of the strongest light London gives. |
| **Closing line** | Tie back to the UVP without echoing Block 2. Single sentence. Carries "London wedding or elopement." |

Target length: 140-180 words. Refined Dreamer voice. Banned-word audit clean. No em dashes. No recycled imagery.
