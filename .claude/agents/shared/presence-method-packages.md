## Pricing framework

The pricing follows a perceived discount model. The base package (The Vow) sets the per-hour anchor at full rate. Each tier up offers more hours at a lower effective rate, creating a perceived discount that makes the middle tiers feel like the obvious choice.

- 0% discount on base — establishes the anchor
- 18-25% discount on middle tiers — creates the "sweet spot"
- 27% discount on top tier — rewards the biggest commitment

The "value" line on each package = base rate x hours (what it *would* cost at The Vow's per-hour rate). The actual price is lower, creating the perceived saving.

## Copy and positioning notes for Jodie

- "The Presence Method" is the named mechanism — use consistently across all copy
- Never sell hours — sell the transformation and the trust
- The pre-wedding shoot is NOT an add-on — it is the mechanism. Frame it as the reason the wedding day works
- Package names follow the emotional arc: Vow (commitment) — Bloom (opening) — Glow (radiance) — Odyssey (adventure)
- Use "commissions" not "packages" in premium-facing copy
- The perceived discount math should never appear on the website — it's internal pricing logic only
- International couples need trust signals early: reviews, response time, process clarity
- The nervousness-to-ease transformation must appear in every piece of sales copy
- Specific client stories (Alex and Juliet, Morgan and Gabby) are more powerful than any descriptor
- Pricing on the website shows "from £X" — full breakdowns happen in the enquiry conversation
- Payment plans available — mention on website, detail in enquiry
