## HADI PHOTOGRAPHY LONDON — 6 Core Objections

---

**Objection 1: "Your prices are higher than other photographers we've looked at."**

*Real concern:* Can't yet see why you're worth more.

*Answer:*
Most photographers turn up on the day and hope for the best. What you're paying for isn't just the hours on the day, it's everything that happens before arrival. The Vision Process. The location briefing. The pre-wedding shoot that means the couple arrives on their wedding morning already knowing how this feels. 18 out of 25 clients described the experience in almost identical words, it felt like spending time with an old friend, not being photographed by a stranger. That doesn't happen by accident. It's a method, and it takes time to do properly.

*Closer:* The question isn't whether this costs more than someone else, it's what the difference in experience is actually worth on a day you can't repeat.

---

**Objection 2: "We're still looking at other photographers."**

*Real concern:* Haven't found their trust signal yet.

*Answer:*
That's exactly the right approach, look carefully. Read the reviews, not just the star ratings. Look for what people said about how the day *felt*, not just how the photos look. If what you find matches what you're hoping for, reach out. If it doesn't, I'll tell you honestly whether we're the right fit. I'd rather you book the right photographer than book me for the wrong reasons.

*Why this works:* Confidence without pressure. Sends them to the reviews, the strongest asset, and positions Hadi as someone who doesn't need the sale.

---

**Objection 3: "We're worried about booking someone we've never met, we're coming from overseas."**

*Real concern:* What if you're not who you appear to be online?

*Answer:*
Every couple in the gallery was exactly where you are now, booking someone they'd never met, in a city they may not know, trusting a person they'd only spoken to online. Over 100 reviews from real couples, many of them international. A detailed pre-session process that begins weeks before the day. And a video consultation before committing to anything, so the couple can hear how I work, ask every question, and decide whether they trust me. I've never had a couple leave that call feeling uncertain.

*Closer:* The process before the day is specifically designed for couples trusting someone from a distance, it's not an afterthought, it's the foundation.

---

**Objection 4: "We're camera-shy and worried we'll look awkward."**

*Real concern:* I've seen bad photos of myself and don't want those to be my wedding photos.

*Answer:*
This is the most common thing I hear, and the couples who say it almost always produce the most natural images. I don't ask them to perform. I give them something real to do, something that shifts their attention from the camera to each other. By the time we reach the strongest locations, they've forgotten I'm there. The nervousness doesn't go away because they become brave. It goes away because the whole thing becomes familiar. That's what the three-phase session is for. And the pre-wedding shoot does that work before the wedding day even begins.

---

**Objection 5: "What if the weather is bad on the day?"**

*Real concern:* Will the whole thing be ruined?

*Answer:*
London weather is part of the deal, every version of it has been shot in. Rain changes the light, it doesn't ruin it. Some of the most striking images come from grey skies and wet stone. What matters is having a plan: knowing which locations have covered areas, knowing the exact timing windows where the light still works, arriving with enough flexibility to adapt. That's built into the briefing process.

---

**Objection 6: "Four to six weeks for delivery feels like a long wait."**

*Real concern:* I'm anxious and want reassurance quickly.

*Answer:*
Within 48 hours of the session, a sneak peek arrives, 5 to 10 fully edited images that capture the heart of the day. That's the immediate payoff. The full gallery takes 4 to 6 weeks because every image is individually edited to the same standard, not batch processed, not outsourced. Expedited delivery is available if needed. Most couples tell me the sneak peek is enough to hold them until the full gallery arrives.

## CLICK AI AGENCY — 7 Core Objections

---

**Objection 1: "What happens if the system goes down?"**

*Real concern:* I can't afford to have my phones go dead.

*Answer:*
The system runs at 99.5% uptime. If anything interrupts the AI, calls are automatically diverted to the main line immediately, no caller hits a dead end, no appointment is lost. The clinic is notified proactively if anything changes. Compare that to what's already there: a receptionist who goes on lunch, calls in sick, or is already on another call. The AI doesn't do any of those things.

*Closer:* You're not replacing a perfect system, you're replacing one that already fails regularly, just in ways you've learned to accept.

---

**Objection 2: "Will it work with our current booking system?"**

*Real concern:* I don't want to rip out everything we already use.

*Answer:*
We build around what's already there. Whether the clinic runs Fresha, Timely, TreatWell, or something else, the first step is a full audit of the current setup before recommending anything. If it integrates cleanly, we connect to it. If there are gaps, we say exactly what they are before any commitment. The existing system isn't replaced, an intelligent layer is added on top that handles what the system can't: answering the phone, managing the conversation, capturing every enquiry that would otherwise fall through.

*Closer:* The audit is free and comes with no obligation, they'll know exactly what's possible before spending a penny.

---

**Objection 3: "We already have receptionists. We don't want to replace people."**

*Real concern:* Worried about ethics or staff pushback.

*Answer:*
This isn't about replacing the team, it's about removing the parts of their job that make them ineffective. The AI handles the repetitive layer: FAQ questions, no-show reminders, handling three things at once. What that leaves the team to do is the work that actually requires a human: complex patient queries, sensitive conversations, moments that need real judgement. Most clinic owners find their staff are happier after implementation. They're doing more meaningful work and fewer repetitive tasks.

*Closer:* The goal isn't a smaller team, it's a better-used one.

---

**Objection 4: "We've tried software solutions before and they didn't work."**

*Real concern:* I've been burned. I spent money on something that promised a lot and delivered nothing.

*Answer:*
Most software solutions replace the calendar, the booking form, the confirmation email, the automated reminder. What they don't replace is the conversation. The call that comes in at 6pm on a Friday. The patient who wants to ask three questions before booking. The enquiry that doesn't fit a booking form. That's the gap, and that's exactly what the voice AI handles. Before building anything, a live demo is run configured for a business like theirs, not a generic product demo, a specific one. If it doesn't solve the actual problem, we say so.

*Closer:* The demo takes twenty minutes and costs nothing, if it doesn't answer the specific problem, we stop there.

---

**Objection 5: "We're not sure our patients will respond well to AI."**

*Real concern:* Will this feel cold or impersonal? Will it damage patient relationships?

*Answer:*
The voice is warm, natural, and trained specifically on the clinic, its treatments, practitioners, and tone. Patients aren't told they're speaking to AI unless the clinic wants them to be. What they experience is a call that's answered immediately, handled knowledgeably, and resolved without being put on hold or transferred twice. Most patients can't tell the difference. The ones who can usually prefer it, because it's faster and more consistent. Patient experience goes up, not down.

*Closer:* The best way to answer this is to hear it, the demo includes a live call so they can judge for themselves.

---

**Objection 6: "The setup fee feels like a big upfront commitment."**

*Real concern:* What if I pay £500-£1,500 and it doesn't work out?

*Answer:*
The setup fee covers the work that makes everything function: training the AI on the specific business, building the call flows, integrating with the booking system, testing everything before it goes live. It's not a deposit, it's the build. A generic AI tool costs nothing to set up because it's generic. What the clinic is paying for is a system that knows their business before it ever answers a call. We don't ask for commitment to setup until after the demo, which is free. They'll see exactly what they're paying for before paying for it.

*Closer:* They're not paying for potential, they're paying for a working system configured for their specific business.

---

**Objection 7: "We need to think about it / discuss with the team."**

*Real concern:* Interested but not sole decision maker, or needs more time to trust this.

*Answer:*
Completely understand, this affects how the clinic operates day to day, so it should be a considered decision. Send a one-page summary of exactly what the system does, what it costs, and what the setup process looks like, something they can share with whoever else needs to see it. Offer a second call with the full team so everyone can ask questions directly. Flag that onboarding slots fill up and keeping momentum without rushing the decision is the goal.

*Closer:* Send the summary today, if it makes sense after that, book the team call and go from there.
