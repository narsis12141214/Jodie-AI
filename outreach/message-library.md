## How agents use this file

This is a **lookup table, not a copy-paste source.** Templates are structural scaffolds, not finished copy.

### Copywriter workflow
1. Campaign Builder provides the segment, touch type, and sequence step
2. Copywriter selects the matching template from this library
3. Copywriter pulls the segment-specific angle guide from `skool-playbook.md` Section 11
4. Copywriter generates the email replacing all bracketed placeholders with researched, specific content
5. Copywriter validates against the Golden Rules checklist (`skool-playbook.md` Section 13)
6. Copywriter passes draft to Zizi review queue OR flags for revision if any check fails

### Closer workflow
1. Reply Classifier tags an incoming reply (positive / objection / not-now / etc.)
2. Closer consults the appropriate section of this library based on the tag
3. For positive replies: use Section 4 (Reply Handling) and follow the Conversation Roadmap
4. For objections: use Section 5 (Objection Responses)
5. For nurture situations: use Section 3 (Nurture Messages)

### Non-negotiable rule
Every generated email must:
- Follow the sentence structure and rhythm of the selected template
- Replace every `[bracketed placeholder]` with specific, researched content
- Incorporate the one sharp detail from the prospect's research notes
- Match the sender persona (hadi@ founder voice vs zizi@ operator voice)
- Pass all seven Golden Rules checks

If the Copywriter cannot produce something that passes all five criteria, it flags the prospect for manual handling rather than producing weak copy.

## 1. Initial Contact Messages

First touch. Short, relevant, curiosity-driven. Goal: spark a reply, not close a deal. One per prospect per campaign.

### 1A — Warm Contact: Reconnection

**When to use:** Past colleagues, old clients, or people Hadi has met before.
**Sender persona:** hadi@
**Framework:** ACA (Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask)
**Template:**

> Hey [Name], it's been a while since we last connected! I saw you're now at [Company], congrats on the move. I've been working on some AI workflows that help [industry] teams cut down repetitive work. Curious if you've been exploring anything like that lately?

**Variables to fill:**
- `[Name]` — first name
- `[Company]` — their current company
- `[industry]` — their industry in their own language (not ours)

---

### 1B — Warm Contact: Event/Shared Context

**When to use:** Someone met at an event, webinar, or mutual group.
**Sender persona:** hadi@ or zizi@ depending on who attended
**Framework:** ACA
**Template:**

> Great meeting you at [Event] last week. Your comment about [specific topic] stuck with me. I've been helping companies solve similar challenges with automation. Want me to send you a 2-minute breakdown of how we approached it?

**Variables to fill:**
- `[Event]` — real event name
- `[specific topic]` — something they actually said (not invented)

**Note:** Only use if a 2-minute breakdown asset exists for the referenced challenge. Otherwise adapt the CTA.

---

### 1C — Cold Contact: Pain Point Observation

**When to use:** A specific trigger is identifiable: job posting, product launch, funding, expansion, recent award, press mention, new location.
**Sender persona:** zizi@
**Framework:** 4T's (Trigger, Think, Third-party, Talk)
**Template:**

> Hi [Name], noticed [Company] is [specific trigger, e.g., hiring 2 new chefs / expanding to Mayfair / just won Best Shisha Lounge]. That usually means [problem, e.g., more booking enquiries than the team can keep up with]. A lot of [peer type, e.g., high-end London restaurants] use lightweight AI workflows to handle that without adding headcount. Want me to send a quick case study?

**Variables to fill:**
- `[Name]` — first name (never "team" or "there")
- `[Company]` — their company name
- `[specific trigger]` — real, recent, verifiable
- `[problem]` — the plausible operational consequence of that trigger
- `[peer type]` — the peer category language that applies
- CTA — can be swapped for "happy to record a 90-second walkthrough showing how" if case study isn't available

---

### 1D — Cold Contact: Offer First

**When to use:** No clear trigger identifiable, leading with value instead.
**Sender persona:** zizi@
**Framework:** Offer-led variant of 4T's
**Template:**

> Hi [Name], I've been putting together short teardown videos showing where [peer type, e.g., premium London restaurants] could reduce [pain point, e.g., missed booking enquiries] by 20-30%. Happy to record a free 90-second one for [Company]. Would you want me to send it?

**Variables to fill:**
- `[Name]` — first name
- `[peer type]` — their peer category
- `[pain point]` — specific to their segment
- `[Company]` — their company name

**Click AI Agency constraint:** Only use this template if the universal teardown video asset exists. As of 21 April 2026, we need to confirm/build this before deploying Template 1D.

---

### 1E — Cold Contact: Iranian-to-Iranian (Click-specific, Persian restaurant segment only)

**When to use:** First-touch to Persian/Iranian restaurant owners. Founder voice required.
**Sender persona:** hadi@ (non-negotiable)
**Framework:** Modified 4T's with cultural/personal opener
**Template:**

> Dear [Mr./Ms. Surname],
>
> Hope you are doing well. I've been a big fan of what you've done with [Restaurant] for a long time. [Specific detail, e.g., the way you brought authentic Tehran kababi to Soho, or the Michelin Bib Gourmand, or the new site opening]. That's seriously impressive.
>
> I'm Iranian too, and honestly, it would be an honour to support a fellow Iranian who's built something this special in London. [Click] helps premium restaurants like [Restaurant] handle all their customer messages, bookings, DMs and calls, no missed enquiries, no lost bookings, no chatbot nonsense. We actually answer real questions properly: menu, allergies, availability, everything.
>
> We've spent two years perfecting this system for venues at your level. Happy to send you a short demo made specifically for [Restaurant], or pop by for a coffee, whatever works best.
>
> Best,
> Hadi

**Variables to fill:**
- `[Mr./Ms. Surname]` — formal address, research correct spelling
- `[Restaurant]` — exact restaurant name
- `[Specific detail]` — must be genuine, researched, and specific to this restaurant (not generic praise)

**Length exception:** This template exceeds the 3-6 sentence Golden Rule because the Iranian-to-Iranian angle requires fuller emotional context. Hadi has approved this exception for Persian segment only. All other templates must stay 3-6 sentences.

**Banned variants:** Do NOT use this template for non-Persian prospects. Do NOT use the phrase "As your regular customer" anywhere. Do NOT claim to have visited if that claim isn't true.

## 2. Follow-Up Messages

Used when initial contact gets no reply. Each follow-up must add new value, never "just bumping this up." Maximum 2-3 follow-ups per prospect, spaced 2-5 days apart.

### 2A — Soft Reminder

**When to use:** 2-3 days after initial contact, no reply yet.
**Sender persona:** Same as initial
**Template:**

> Hi [Name], just wanted to follow up in case my note slipped by. Happy to share a quick example of how [peer company] approached [pain point]. Would that be helpful?

**Variables to fill:**
- `[Name]` — first name
- `[peer company]` — real example from our case studies (Rendezvous for restaurants, Kailee clinic for aesthetics) or relevant published case study
- `[pain point]` — segment-specific

---

### 2B — Value Drop

**When to use:** 3-5 days after 2A. Earn attention with something tangible.
**Sender persona:** Same as initial
**Template:**

> Hi [Name], I pulled a resource that might be relevant: [short description of genuinely useful artifact]. It's a quick way [peer type] avoid [common mistake]. Would you like me to send it over?

**Variables to fill:**
- `[Name]` — first name
- `[short description]` — must reference a real artifact (checklist, short guide, case study, short video). Do not promise artifacts that don't exist.
- `[peer type]` — their segment
- `[common mistake]` — specific operational mistake in their segment

**Click AI Agency constraint:** Copywriter must verify artifact referenced actually exists in the Click AI Agency asset library before sending.

---

### 2C — Permission Close (the "breakup email")

**When to use:** ~1 week after 2B. Final message after 2-3 unanswered attempts.
**Sender persona:** Same as initial
**Template:**

> Hi [Name], happy to close the loop if now's not the right time. If [pain point/outcome] becomes a priority down the road, I can send over a short video that shows what's working. Should I check back later this quarter?

**Variables to fill:**
- `[Name]` — first name
- `[pain point/outcome]` — specific to their segment

**Purpose:** This email often gets replies from prospects who were busy but interested. It also cleanly closes the file on uninterested prospects. Don't skip it.

## 3. Nurture Messages

For prospects who engaged once but aren't ready to buy, or past clients to keep warm. Cadence: 1-2 per month maximum. Each must add genuine value, not be a sales push.

### 3A — Insight Share

**When to use:** You came across a stat, article, or idea genuinely tied to their industry.
**Sender persona:** hadi@ for founder-level relationships, zizi@ for operational
**Template:**

> Hey [Name], thought of you when I saw this: [insight/stat + source]. It reminded me of our chat about [topic]. Want me to show you how one of our clients solved it?

**Variables to fill:**
- `[Name]` — first name
- `[insight/stat + source]` — real, recent, cite the source
- `[topic]` — something genuinely discussed previously

**Constraint:** Never fabricate a "chat we had" that didn't happen.

---

### 3B — Congratulatory Note

**When to use:** They got promoted, launched something, raised funding, won an award, opened a new site.
**Sender persona:** hadi@ preferred
**Template:**

> Congrats on [specific achievement], [Name]! That's huge. I've seen other [peer type] at that stage get swamped with [problem]. When the time's right, I'd be glad to share how we helped a team in a similar spot.

**Variables to fill:**
- `[specific achievement]` — real and recent
- `[Name]` — first name
- `[peer type]` — their peer category
- `[problem]` — the operational problem that typically scales with their achievement

---

### 3C — Check-In With Value

**When to use:** Re-engaging after weeks or months of silence.
**Sender persona:** Same as original relationship holder
**Template:**

> Hi [Name], hope all's well! I just wrapped a project for a [industry] team that [specific outcome, e.g., cut missed bookings by 60%]. Thought of you since we'd discussed [topic] before. Want me to send over a 2-minute recap?

**Variables to fill:**
- `[Name]` — first name
- `[industry]` — their industry
- `[specific outcome]` — real outcome from a real client project
- `[topic]` — genuinely discussed previously

---

### 3D — Ask for Guidance/Referral

**When to use:** If they're not the decision-maker, or we need an introduction.
**Sender persona:** zizi@
**Template:**

> Hi [Name], I know this may not be your focus directly, but I've been helping [industry] teams with [pain point]. Is there someone on your team I should reach out to about this?

**Variables to fill:**
- `[Name]` — first name
- `[industry]` — their industry
- `[pain point]` — specific operational pain

## 4. Reply Handling (used by Closer agent)

Used when a prospect replies positively to an outreach email. Follows the Conversation Roadmap from `skool-playbook.md` Section 8.

### 4A — "Send me more info" response

**When to use:** Prospect asks for more information.
**Template:**

> Great! Here's a short overview that shows the workflow in action: [link]. If it looks useful, would you be open to a quick call this week so I can walk you through how it would work specifically for [Company]?

**Constraint:** Do not send generic marketing collateral. The overview should be specific to their segment.

---

### 4B — "How does this work?" response

**When to use:** Prospect asks how the system functions.
**Template:**

> Short version: it's a lightweight AI system that handles customer enquiries, bookings, and DMs automatically, while keeping the tone and experience of your venue intact. The best way to see if it's useful is to look at your specific setup, want to jump on a quick 15-min call so I can tailor what it'd look like for [Company]?

**Constraint:** Avoid jargon. No "LLM," "MCP server," "n8n," "workflows." The prospect doesn't care about the stack.

---

### 4C — "Sure, send the demo" response

**When to use:** Prospect agrees to see the demo.
**Template:**

> Perfect. I'll put something together specifically for [Company], should take me a day or two. In the meantime, is there anything specific you'd like the demo to cover (booking flow, DM handling, social content, all of the above)?

**Operational note:** This triggers a custom demo build. Hadi must be notified so the build is queued. Don't promise a timeline shorter than two working days.

---

### 4D — "I'm interested, let's chat" response

**When to use:** Prospect wants a call directly.
**Template:**

> Brilliant. Here's my calendar: [link]. Grab any 15-min slot that works. If nothing fits, let me know a couple of times that do and I'll make one of them work.

**Operational note:** Use Hadi's personal Calendly. Do not propose times manually; always send the scheduling link.

## 5. Objection Responses

Used by Closer agent when Reply Classifier tags a reply as "objection." Most objections are buying signals in disguise.

### 5A — Budget objection

**Prospect says:** "We don't have budget."
**Response:**

> Totally understand. Most of the venues we work with started with a 30-day free pilot to prove ROI before committing to anything. Would it help if I shared what that looks like?

---

### 5B — Existing tool objection

**Prospect says:** "We already have a system for that."
**Response:**

> Makes sense, most venues do. The way we fit in is by connecting to what you already use rather than replacing it, so the team doesn't have to learn a new tool. Want me to show you how that looks in practice?

---

### 5C — Timing objection

**Prospect says:** "Not right now" / "Maybe later."
**Response:**

> No worries at all. Would it be helpful if I checked back in next quarter? In the meantime, happy to send you a short overview so you've got it when the timing's right.

---

### 5D — "Send me your pricing" (reply before interest established)

**When to use:** Prospect skips past qualification and asks for pricing directly.
**Response:**

> Happy to, pricing depends a bit on setup and volume, so it's usually easier to show you what's included in a 10-min chat. Otherwise I can send a rough range if that helps you decide if it's worth the call?

**Purpose:** Respects their time while keeping the qualification step. Don't send pricing cold, it rarely lands well without context.

## 8. Golden Rules (mirror from playbook, always enforced)

Every email generated must pass these seven checks:

1. **One clear objective** and one simple CTA
2. **3-6 sentences**, plain text, mobile-friendly (exception: Template 1E only)
3. **One true research-derived detail** that proves homework
4. **Offer is specific and low-friction** — video, audit, or snippet they can consume
5. **No banned phrases or patterns** (see `skool-playbook.md` Section 12)
6. **Follow-ups scheduled** with fresh value adds
7. **Tracking enabled** — logged with sender, timestamp, segment, sequence step

If any check fails, Copywriter flags for revision rather than passing to review queue.
