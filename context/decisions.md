[2026-06-08] DECISION: Banned-word rule for photography copy RECALIBRATED. The list from Maddie Mae's homepage template (authentic, timeless, intentional, moments, memories, capture, telling your story, unforgettable, documentary, stress-free, intimate, meaningful, unique, personal/personalized, connection, "not your average photographer", "more than just a photographer", wild at heart, "for the passionately in love", adventurous, modern romantic, "capture how it felt", "you deserve") is NOT an absolute prohibition list. The rule is: don't lean on these as cliché filler crutches. Use them ONLY when they're the most accurate word for what you're describing. Maddie's own website uses these words intentionally. The vigilance is against generic, easy, lazy use, not against accurate vocabulary. | REASONING: Hadi reviewed Maddie Mae's actual website and confirmed she uses these "banned" words intentionally and accurately. The earlier rule (absolute prohibition + Control-F check before any draft clears) was a misread of Maddie's guidance. Removing accurate vocabulary from a copywriter's toolkit damages precision more than it helps. Reframing as "anti-cliché crutch" rather than "anti-vocabulary" preserves the spirit of Maddie's warning (don't sound like every other London wedding photographer) without crippling word choice. | CONTEXT: Applies to all Phase 2 homepage drafting, all future photography customer-facing copy (inquiry replies, captions, pricing pages, contact pages, contracts, signatures), blog cadence restart, and any rewrites of existing pages.
